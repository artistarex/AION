# Newton Protocols
## 03_Institutes / Newton / Protocols.md

---

## Protocol N-01: Research Statement Mandatory

No Newton research begins without an approved Research Statement.
Format per `06_Templates/Research_Template.md`.
Archimedes approves before any investigation starts.

---

## Protocol N-02: The Reproducibility Requirement

Every Newton output must include enough methodological detail
that an independent agent could reproduce the process and verify the result.

"I analyzed the data" is not reproducible.
"I applied Pearson correlation to variables X and Y across n=247 data points" is reproducible.

---

## Protocol N-03: Uncertainty Is Mandatory

All Newton quantitative outputs include:
- Sample size (where applicable)
- Confidence level or interval
- Known limitations of the method
- Conditions under which the finding may not hold

Stating certainty without acknowledging uncertainty is a Newton protocol violation.

---

## Protocol N-04: Peer Review for Significant Outputs

Any Newton output that will be promoted to the Knowledge Base or delivered to an operator
must be reviewed by a second Newton agent before submission to Archimedes.

Ptolemy reviews Euclid's mathematical outputs.
Socrates reviews Curie's experimental designs.
Mendel reviews any output before archiving.

---

## Protocol N-05: Verification Before Archiving

No Newton finding enters the Knowledge Base without passing through Socrates' verification.
Mendel does not archive unverified findings.
Unverified findings are clearly labeled in working documents.

---

## Protocol N-06: Source Quality Hierarchy

Newton agents apply this source priority order:

1. Peer-reviewed primary research (highest)
2. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses
3. Institutional scientific reports
4. Textbooks and academic references
5. Secondary sources with clear primary citations
6. Unverified sources (used with explicit labeling only)

A lower-tier source never overrides a higher-tier source without documented justification.
