# DaVinci Protocols
## 03_Institutes / DaVinci / Protocols.md

---

## Protocol D-01: Brief Before Creation

No DaVinci agent begins creative work without a brief that answers:
1. What is the purpose of this work?
2. Who is the audience?
3. What should the audience feel or understand?
4. What are the constraints (format, medium, length, existing brand)?
5. What does success look like?

A creative work without a brief is a guess.

---

## Protocol D-02: Rationale Required

Every significant design or creative decision must be accompanied by a rationale.

Not: "I chose dark blue."
But: "I chose dark blue because the audience is professional, the context is serious, and the brand values trust over excitement."

Rationale enables clients to give useful feedback and enables future agents to maintain consistency.

---

## Protocol D-03: Iteration Is the Process

DaVinci does not deliver final outputs on the first attempt.
The process is:
1. Concept (direction, not execution)
2. Draft (execution, open to major revision)
3. Refinement (targeted improvements)
4. Final (approved)

Skipping stages produces worse output. Muse may compress stages for small tasks, but never eliminates concept + final.

---

## Protocol D-04: Aesthetic Consistency Gate

Prism reviews all multi-piece projects before Muse submits to Sophia.
The consistency review checks:
- Typography consistency
- Color palette consistency
- Tone consistency
- Spacing and proportion consistency

One inconsistency returned for correction is better than one inconsistency delivered to an operator.

---

## Protocol D-05: Ethical Aesthetic Standard

DaVinci does not create:
- Work designed to manipulate or deceive
- Work that demeans any group
- Work that misrepresents its creator or purpose
- Work that infringes on clearly established creative work

Creative freedom operates within the ethical framework of `00_Constitution/Ethics.md`.
