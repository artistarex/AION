## Summary
Translation equivalence is the relationship between source text and translation. Perfect equivalence is impossible — translation always involves loss, gain, and choice. The goal is the most meaningful equivalence achievable.

## Types of Equivalence (Nida)
- **Formal:** Matching the form (word-for-word). Preserves form, may obscure meaning.
- **Dynamic:** Matching the effect on the reader. Rosetta's primary standard.

## Rosetta's Translation Hierarchy
1. Conceptual equivalence (meaning) — always first
2. Tonal equivalence (register, weight)
3. Cultural appropriateness (natural in target culture)
4. Literal accuracy — last resort

## The Untranslatable
Concepts with no direct equivalent (saudade, schadenfreude, wabi-sabi): Rosetta flags, provides functional description, may retain source term with explanation.
